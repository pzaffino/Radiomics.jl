First-order Features

First-order features describe the statistical properties of voxel intensities within a region of interest (ROI). These features do not consider the spatial arrangement of voxels but only the distribution of intensity values.

Features calculated in Radiomics.jl:

  • Energy: sum of squared voxel intensities.
  • Total energy: energy multiplied by the voxel volume.
  • Entropy: a measure of uncertainty or randomness in the voxel intensity distribution.
  • Minimum / Maximum: the minimum and maximum voxel values within the ROI.
  • 10th / 90th percentile: intensity values corresponding to the 10th and 90th percentiles.
  • Mean / Median: average and median of voxel values.
  • Interquartile range: difference between the 75th and 25th percentiles.
  • Range: difference between the maximum and minimum voxel values.
  • Mean absolute deviation: average absolute deviation from the mean.
  • Robust mean absolute deviation: mean absolute deviation calculated from values between the 10th and 90th percentiles.
  • Root mean squared: square root of the mean of squared voxel values.
  • Standard deviation: standard deviation of voxel values.
  • Skewness: measure of asymmetry of the voxel intensity distribution.
  • Kurtosis: measure of the "tailedness" of the distribution.
  • Variance: variance of voxel values.
  • Uniformity: measure of the uniformity of voxel intensities (higher = more uniform).

These features provide quantitative information about the texture of medical images within the ROI and are essential for radiomic analysis.
