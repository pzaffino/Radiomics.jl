Gray Level Co-occurrence Matrix (GLCM) feature extraction in Julia

GLCM Feature Extraction

This page documents the main functions for computing 2D and 3D GLCM features.

Key Features

  • Autocorrelation: measures correlation between intensities of neighboring voxels.
  • Cluster Prominence: indicates sharpness of deviations from the mean; detects asymmetric patterns.
  • Cluster Shade: measures asymmetry of cluster distributions.
  • Cluster Tendency: degree of clustering of similar values.
  • Contrast: measures local intensity contrast between neighboring voxels.
  • Correlation: statistical correlation between intensities of neighboring voxels.
  • Joint Energy: uniformity of the matrix; high values indicate homogeneity.
  • Joint Entropy: measures the complexity or disorder of the GLCM.
  • Inverse Difference Moment (IDM): measures homogeneity; higher values indicate less local variation.
  • Normalized IDM (IDMN): normalized version of IDM.
  • Inverse Difference (ID): similar to IDM, measures proximity of values to central voxel.
  • Normalized Inverse Difference (IDN): normalized version of ID.
  • Inverse Variance: measures dispersion of GLCM values.
  • Maximum Probability: maximum co-occurrence probability between gray levels.
  • Sum of Squares: measures total variation relative to the mean.
  • Difference Average: mean of intensity differences between neighboring voxels.
  • Difference Entropy: disorder of intensity differences.
  • Difference Variance: variance of intensity differences between neighbors.
  • Sum Average: mean of sums of GLCM value pairs.
  • Sum Entropy: entropy of sums; measures complexity.
  • Joint Average: global mean of GLCM values.
  • IMC1 / IMC2: measures of informational correlation between marginal distributions.
  • Maximal Correlation Coefficient (MCC): measures maximum linear correlation between rows and columns of the GLCM.

Notes

These functions are designed for advanced radiomics analysis on medical images. The 2D wrapper allows the 3D calculation to be applied to planar images by adding a singleton dimension.
