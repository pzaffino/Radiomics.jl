Gray Level Dependence Matrix (GLDM) feature extraction in Julia

GLDM Feature Extraction

GLDM features quantify the spatial dependence of gray levels in an image, measuring how many neighboring voxels share similar intensity values.

Key Features

  • Small Dependence Emphasis (SDE): highlights fine textures.
  • Large Dependence Emphasis (LDE): emphasizes coarse textures.
  • Gray Level Non-Uniformity (GLNU): measures variability of gray levels.
  • Dependence Non-Uniformity (DNU): measures variability of dependence sizes.
  • Dependence Non-Uniformity Normalized (DNUN): normalized DNU.
  • Gray Level Variance (GLV): variance of gray levels.
  • Dependence Variance (DV): variance of dependence sizes.
  • Dependence Entropy (DE): randomness of voxel dependencies.
  • Low/High Gray Level Emphasis (LGL/HGL): emphasizes low/high intensity values.
  • Small/Large Dependence Low/High Gray Level Emphasis (SD/LG-L/HGL): joint measures of dependence size and gray levels.

Calculation Steps

  1. Discretize the image into gray levels using n_bins or bin_width.
  2. Compute the GLDM matrix with calculate_gldm_matrix, counting the number of dependent neighbors for each gray level.
  3. Compute coefficients with calculate_gldm_coefficients (including pd, pg, ivector, jvector, and total zones Nz).
  4. Calculate features using their corresponding formulas, e.g., SDE, LDE, GLNU, DV, DE, etc.

Notes

GLDM features are widely used in radiomics to capture texture patterns in 3D medical images, providing measures of homogeneity, coarseness, and gray level distribution.
