3D Shape Features

The get_shape3d_features function computes geometric descriptors of 3D objects in medical images. The input is a 3D binary mask and voxel spacing. The function can keep only the largest connected component and pads the mask for accurate surface extraction.

Key Features

  • Surface Area: Area of the 3D object's surface mesh.
  • Mesh Volume: Volume calculated from the surface mesh.
  • Surface-to-Volume Ratio: Ratio of surface area to volume.
  • Sphericity: Degree of similarity to a sphere.
  • Maximum 3D Diameter: Largest distance between any two surface points (Feret diameter).
  • Principal Axes Lengths: Major, minor, and least axis lengths derived from the covariance matrix.
  • Elongation: Ratio between middle and largest principal axes.
  • Flatness: Ratio between smallest and largest principal axes.
  • Voxel-based Volume: Volume computed by counting foreground voxels and scaling with spacing.

Methodology

Extraction relies on the Marching Cubes algorithm for surface mesh generation and linear algebra operations on voxel coordinates for shape descriptors. Optional sampling allows fast approximation of maximum 3D diameter for large volumes.
