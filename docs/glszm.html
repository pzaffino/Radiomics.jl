Gray Level Size Zone Matrix (GLSZM) feature extraction in Julia

GLSZM Feature Extraction

This page documents the main functions for computing GLSZM features, which quantify the size of homogeneous zones for each gray level in a region of interest (ROI).

Key Features

  • Small Area Emphasis (SAE): measures the distribution of small homogeneous zones; higher values indicate finer textures.
  • Large Area Emphasis (LAE): measures the distribution of large homogeneous zones; higher values indicate coarser textures.
  • Gray Level Non-Uniformity (GLN): measures the variability of gray levels; lower values indicate more uniform gray levels.
  • Gray Level Non-Uniformity Normalized (GLNN): normalized version of GLN.
  • Size Zone Non-Uniformity (SZN): measures variability of zone sizes; lower values indicate more uniform zone sizes.
  • Size Zone Non-Uniformity Normalized (SZNN): normalized version of SZN.
  • Zone Percentage (ZP): ratio of number of zones to total number of voxels; indicates texture density.
  • Gray Level Variance (GLV): variance of gray level intensities.
  • Zone Variance (ZV): variance of zone sizes.
  • Zone Entropy (ZE): measures randomness in zone distribution.
  • Low Gray Level Zone Emphasis (LGLZE): emphasizes zones with low gray levels.
  • High Gray Level Zone Emphasis (HGLZE): emphasizes zones with high gray levels.
  • Small Area Low Gray Level Emphasis (SALGLE): emphasizes small zones with low gray levels.
  • Small Area High Gray Level Emphasis (SAHGLE): emphasizes small zones with high gray levels.
  • Large Area Low Gray Level Emphasis (LALGLE): emphasizes large zones with low gray levels.
  • Large Area High Gray Level Emphasis (LAHGLE): emphasizes large zones with high gray levels.

Calculation Steps

  1. Discretize the image based on n_bins or bin_width.
  2. Compute the GLSZM matrix with calculate_glszm_matrix.
  3. Compute coefficients using calculate_glszm_coefficients.
  4. Calculate each feature using its corresponding formula, e.g., small_area_emphasis, zone_entropy, etc.

Notes

GLSZM features are particularly useful for characterizing texture patterns in medical images, capturing both the size and intensity distribution of homogeneous zones.
